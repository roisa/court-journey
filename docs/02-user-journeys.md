# Phase 2 — User Journey Mapping

We map the same three-act structure (**Before → During → After**) across the player's lifecycle. The point is to show how the *same app* must mean *different things* as the player matures. The app grows up with the user.

A guiding idea: **early on, the app holds your hand and calms your nerves. Later, it holds your memories and shows you your growth.** The emotional center of gravity shifts from *anxiety* to *nostalgia*.

---

## Journey 1 — The First Tournament Ever 😰

**Emotional state:** excited but terrified. Impostor syndrome. "Do I even belong here?"
**App's job:** reduce fear, set expectations, capture a milestone they'll treasure forever.

### Before
- **Onboarding (≤90s):** sport, level (self-described in plain words — "I rally fine but matches scare me"), and *why* they're here ("I want to remember", "I want to improve", "I want to feel ready").
- **First-Tournament Checklist** auto-appears (Phase 7): not 40 items — ~7 reassuring ones. ("Pack 2 rackets," "Eat 2h before," "It's normal to be nervous — everyone is.")
- **Expectation reset:** a gentle card: *"Goal for your first tournament: have fun and finish. Winning is a bonus."* This single message prevents the #1 churn event — quitting after a first-round loss.

### During
- **Zero pressure to use the app mid-event.** Maybe one prompt: "Take a photo of the court / your draw sheet." That's it. No live scoring.
- A pre-match **60-second calm card**: 3 breaths, one cue ("watch the ball, move your feet"), one mantra.

### After
- **First Reflection** (Phase 6): 3 taps + optional 20-sec voice note. Win or lose.
- **The magic:** AI turns it into their *first journal entry* — a warm, narrative recap with the photo. "Your tennis story has begun."
- **First achievement unlocked:** "Day One — you showed up." (Showing up *is* the achievement.)
- If they lost: reframing copy — *"Everybody loses their first tournament match. The players who keep a journal are the ones who come back better."*

**Success metric:** they finish the first reflection and feel *proud*, not *embarrassed*. They come back for tournament #2.

---

## Journey 2 — The Fifth Tournament 🎯

**Emotional state:** comfortable with the format, now *competitive*. Wants to win, starts noticing patterns. "Why do I always lose the long ones?"
**App's job:** shift from hand-holding to **learning and confidence**.

### Before
- **Regular Tournament Checklist** — now trimmed; the app remembers what they always pack and hides solved items.
- **"Last time here" recall:** "You played this club in March — you noted the courts were fast. Bring your control racket?"
- **Pre-tournament confidence card:** AI surfaces *one* relevant lesson from past reflections: *"Your last 2 losses came from rushing on big points. Your cue: slow your feet, not your swing."*

### During
- Light capture: a photo, a quick voice memo between matches ("that backhand return felt unreal").
- Optional **moment marker**: one tap to flag "something special just happened" — fill it in later.

### After
- Reflection now feels *valuable*, not just sentimental — they start to see the Improvement Engine connect dots (Phase 8): "3rd time you've mentioned your second serve under pressure."
- **First real insight delivered:** "Wow, I didn't realize that." Hook deepens.
- Rivalry tracking emerges naturally: "You've now played Marco 3 times (1–2)."

**Success metric:** the user trusts the app's insights and *acts* on one before tournament #6.

---

## Journey 3 — The Twentieth Tournament 🏆

**Emotional state:** identity fully formed — "I'm a tournament player." Has wins, losses, rivals, scars, comebacks. Sometimes plateaus or loses motivation.
**App's job:** **nostalgia, identity, and re-motivation.** The archive is now the treasure.

### Before
- Checklist is basically automatic — one glance.
- **"You've been here before" deep recall:** full history vs. this draw, this venue, this rival. Confidence through evidence: *"You've won 6 of your last 8 opening matches. You start well."*
- High-stakes mode if it matters (Phase 7): mental routine they've personally proven works ("Your best tournaments all started with the same warm-up playlist + arriving 45 min early").

### During
- Effortless capture, now mostly muscle memory. Photos auto-collect into the tournament.

### After
- Reflection is a cherished ritual. The recap entry slots into a **deep timeline** spanning years.
- **Yearly Recap & Documentary** (Phase 10) becomes the emotional peak of their year.
- Re-motivation engine kicks in during plateaus: *"A year ago you were stuck too — then you won 3 in a row. You've broken through before."*
- The app celebrates milestones they'd never track themselves: "Your 100th match," "3 years on court," "You've now played in 7 different cities."

**Success metric:** during a motivation dip, the app pulls them back. The archive is irreplaceable; churn is near-zero.

---

## How the experience evolves (the throughline)

| Dimension | 1st tournament | 5th | 20th |
|-----------|---------------|-----|------|
| Dominant emotion | Fear | Ambition | Pride / nostalgia |
| App's primary role | Calm + capture milestone | Coach + confidence | Memory keeper + re-motivator |
| Checklist | Long, reassuring | Trimmed, smart | Near-automatic |
| Reflection value | Sentimental | Insightful | Treasured ritual |
| Insights | None yet | First patterns | Deep, predictive |
| What keeps them | Novelty + pride | Improvement | The archive itself |
| Notifications | Encouragement | Lessons | Memories & milestones |

**The arc:** *Hold my hand → Coach me → Remember me.* Same app, three relationships. An app that nails only one of these loses the user at the transition. The design must make each handoff feel like the app *grew with you*, not like a different product.
