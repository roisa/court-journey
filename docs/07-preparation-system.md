# Phase 7 — Tournament Preparation System

Prep is where the app converts **anxiety → confidence**. It's the second emotional transformation (alongside Reflection's disappointment → growth) and a major reason users open the app *before* events.

**Core principle:** a checklist isn't about logistics — it's about *peace of mind*. The real deliverable is the feeling of *"I've done everything I can; I can relax now."* Every item should reduce a worry.

---

## The adaptive philosophy

The system reads the player's **experience level** (tournaments played) and the **stakes** (which they set), then shows the *right amount*. Beginners need reassurance and detail; veterans need a glance. **The checklist shrinks as you grow** — visible proof of your own progress, and a delight in itself.

```
Experience  →  Checklist length & tone
Beginner       Long, reassuring, explains WHY
Intermediate   Trimmed, smart recall, fewer items
Veteran        Near-automatic, one glance, only the personal essentials
```

Items the player reliably packs/does are **learned and auto-hidden** ("you always bring this — we'll stop asking"). The list earns the user's trust by getting out of the way.

---

## 1. First Tournament Checklist 😰

Goal: *reduce fear, set expectations.* ~7–9 items, warm tone, each with a one-line "why."

- 🎒 **Two rackets** — "A string can break. Don't let it end your day."
- 💧 **Water + a snack** — "Matches run long. Energy = focus."
- 🍌 **Eat ~2 hours before** — "Not too close to match time."
- 🧴 **Sunscreen / hat / extra shirt** — "Comfort = confidence."
- 🕐 **Arrive 30–45 min early** — "Rushing kills your nerves."
- 📋 **Know the format** — "Best of 3? Tiebreak? Ask if unsure — everyone does."
- 🧘 **Reframe the nerves** — "Everyone here is nervous. Goal #1: have fun and finish."
- 📸 **Take one photo** — "You'll want to remember your first one."
- 🎯 **Pick ONE thing to focus on** — "e.g. 'watch the ball.' Simplicity wins."

Plus a **pre-match calm card** (3 breaths, 1 cue, 1 mantra).

---

## 2. Regular Tournament Checklist 🎯

Goal: *efficient readiness for the experienced-but-not-veteran player.* ~5–6 items, plus personalization.

- 🎒 Gear check (auto-collapsed if you always pack the same)
- 💧 Hydration & nutrition plan
- 🕐 Arrival time (pulled from "what worked best for you last time")
- 🧠 **Your confidence card** — AI surfaces *one* relevant past lesson (Phase 8): *"Last 2 losses: rushed on big points. Cue: slow feet."*
- 📍 **Venue recall** — "You've played here. You noted the courts are fast."
- 🎯 One tactical intention for the event

---

## 3. High-Stakes Tournament Checklist 🏆

Goal: *peak readiness + proven mental routine* for events that matter (player flags it as high-stakes). Adds a mental/preparation layer on top of the regular list.

- 🛌 **Sleep & taper** — "2 nights of good sleep matters more than 1."
- 🍽 **Your proven pre-tournament routine** — AI reconstructs it: *"Your best results all started with: arrive 45 min early, your warm-up playlist, 10-min hit, banana at -30 min."*
- 🧘 **Mental routine** — a personalized pressure script built from past wins ("In your 3 best comebacks, you stayed point-by-point and breathed between points").
- 📹 **Scout intention** (light) — "Anything you remember about this opponent/draw?" (pulls rivalry history if it exists)
- 🎯 **One game-plan sentence** — not a tactics essay. One sentence.
- 💬 **Affirmation from your own history** — *"You've won 6 of your last 8 opening matches. You start strong."* (confidence from evidence, not platitudes)

---

## 4. Custom Checklist 🛠

Goal: *let the player own it.* 

- Add/remove/reorder any item; save personal items (e.g. "tape for my wrist," "lucky wristband").
- Save a custom list as a reusable **template** ("My clay setup," "My indoor setup").
- The app *suggests* additions based on patterns ("You mentioned blisters twice — add 'blister tape'?") but never forces them.

---

## How the system adapts (the engine)

| Signal | Adaptation |
|--------|-----------|
| Tournaments played (count) | Shorter list, less explanation, more trust |
| Stakes (user-set: casual / regular / high) | Adds mental & routine layers |
| Past reflections (Phase 8) | Injects the *one* most relevant confidence cue |
| Venue history | "You've been here before" recall |
| Rivalry history | Opponent context (if available) |
| Items consistently checked | Auto-hidden ("you've got this handled") |
| Recurring problems mentioned | Gentle suggested additions |

> **Anti-pattern we avoid:** a giant static checklist that's identical for everyone forever. That's the spreadsheet feeling in disguise. The list must feel *alive and personal*, and it must **shrink over time** — the shrinking list is itself a motivating sign of growth ("Remember when prepping took 20 items? Now it's 4.").

**The completion payoff:** finishing the checklist ends with a confidence moment, not a checkmark — *"You're ready. Go enjoy it. 🎾"* The point was never the list. It was the calm.
